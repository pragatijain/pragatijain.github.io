Documentation Projects

JustPet Web Application

Context

The Applied Computing Foundation (ACF) is an education-based non-profit organization that teaches technology and entrepreneurship to middle and high school students through its ACF Start-up Team program.

Opportunity

As a part-time ACF start-up coach, I coached students for four seasons (one season is fourteen weeks) to develop their entrepreneurship, technical, and community outreach skills while identifying and solving a real-world problem.

Students' ultimate goals are to participate in competitions such as the Congressional App Challenge and Conrad Challenge, and build their college admission portfolio to share their growth stories and accomplishments.

Contribution

As the JustPet ACF start-up coach, over a four-season cycle I mentored the students to:

  • Think critically and research a real-world problem scenario before designing the solution. For example, how can we reduce the carbon footprint of pets? What are pet owner pain points for recycling their gently owned pet accessories?
  • Code the JustPet web application using HTML, CSS, Javascript, Fetch API, and Google Maps API.
  • Test their product, design surveys, and collate feedback for an improved user experience.
  • Publish content for either the project website, blogs, or social media to improve the product awareness and to identify the needs of the target audience.
  • Reflect on their accomplishments, challenges, improvements, and aspirations to set the team's goals for the next season.
  • Submit the JustPet web application to the Congressional App Challenge.

Impact

  • Developed Fetch API tutorials to support classroom learnings of other ACF start-up teams.
  • Conceptualized the engineering notebook, and mentored other ACF coaches on how to use it in their classrooms to manage their student learnings. It is now a standard part of the ACF classrooms.
